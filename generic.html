My
Story

My name is Fabio Hinojosa Jimenez,
I was born in Cochabamba, Bolivia where I spent almost all my life.
I moved to the U.S. in 2021 to pursue my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science

I consider myself an active and curious person who loves to learn as much as possible on a daily basis. I am currently pursuing my Bachelor's in Computer Science and expect to graduate in December 2024. Throughout my four years of college, I have been involved in a vast array of groups, clubs, and communities. I served as the president of the robotics club and the ACM Chapter, participating in various competitions, including the International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC), NASA Lunabotics, National Cyber League (NCL), and many others.

Because of my good grades and dedication, I was also given the opportunity to work as a Computer Peer Tutor (CPT), a job for which I am very thankful and happy. In this role, I tutored students with their programming homework and organized events like game jams and workshops, with the purpose of engaging students and helping them learn more about the vast world of computer science. While doing all this, I still found time to participate in the volleyball club, traveling to regional and national championships throughout the semester. This engagement allowed me to meet new professors who provided me with the opportunity to work as a research assistant, creating a VR cybersecurity training program for new IT hires. Additionally, I worked on AI and computer vision technologies in my second research assistant position.

My hard work reflects the passion I have for computer science and for helping people by creating solutions using the knowledge I acquire in school. In Bolivia, I had a vision to help not only my community but as many people as I could. Coming to the U.S., I wanted nothing more than to make that vision a reality. My dream is to continue pursuing this passion and to keep helping people while doing what I love.
